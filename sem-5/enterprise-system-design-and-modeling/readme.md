# SECP3744 - Enterprise Systems Design and Modeling

## 📌 Course Overview
This course focuses on the design and modeling of enterprise-level systems used in large organizations. It emphasizes system integration, data flow, and business process modeling in real-world industrial environments.

---

## 🎯 Learning Outcomes
- Design enterprise-level systems
- Analyze business processes
- Model system architecture
- Understand system integration concepts

---

## 📚 Topics Covered
- Enterprise architecture
- System integration
- Data flow modeling
- Business process modeling
- System design principles

---

## 🛠 Skills Developed
- System modeling
- Architectural design thinking
- Enterprise system analysis
- Problem decomposition

## 📖 Reflection
This subject provided valuable exposure to how large-scale enterprise systems are designed and managed within organizations. I learned that enterprise systems are not only about software development but also about integrating multiple business processes and ensuring smooth data flow across departments. Through modeling exercises, I developed the ability to visualize complex systems and break them down into manageable components.

One of the most important insights gained from this course was understanding how system architecture impacts scalability, maintainability, and performance. As a data engineering student, this knowledge is highly relevant because modern data systems often involve distributed architectures and integrated pipelines. This subject improved my analytical thinking, system design skills, and ability to work with complex real-world system requirements.
